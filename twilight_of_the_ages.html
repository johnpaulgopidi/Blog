The Ticking Clock: A 70 Weeks Timeline

God’s promise to Daniel covers 70 weeks, a complete plan to bring His people back to Him and make everything right. This timeline includes the past, present, and future, all centered on Jesus.

Seventy weeks have been decreed for your people and your holy city, to finish the wrongdoing, to make an end of sin, to make atonement for guilt, to bring in everlasting righteousness, to seal up vision and prophecy, and to anoint the Most Holy Place.
– Daniel 9:24 NASB

This promise is about hope: ending sin, forgiving guilt, and bringing God’s eternal righteousness. It’s a reminder that God has a purpose for every moment, and He invites us to trust Him.

What Has Already Happened

The Messiah’s Coming and Sacrifice

The first part of God’s plan has already come true. Jesus, the Messiah, came to save us, just as Daniel’s prophecy foretold.

Then after the sixty-two weeks, the Messiah will be cut off and have nothing, and the people of the prince who is to come will destroy the city and the sanctuary. And its end will come with a flood; even to the end there will be war; desolations are determined.
– Daniel 9:26 NASB

Jesus came, lived among us, and gave His life on the cross to forgive our sins. After His death, Jerusalem and its temple were destroyed, just as God said. But through Jesus’ sacrifice, we find hope and new life.

For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have everlasting life.
– John 3:16 NKJV

What Is Still to Come

The final week of Daniel’s prophecy is still to come. I believe it points to future events in which God will bring His plan to completion. What I’m sharing here is simply my current understanding. I’ve grouped the Scriptures into sections, not to form a new interpretation, but only to help us see this as a collection of what the Bible itself says. One thing is certain: when the Lord returns, He will bring an end to sin and that will mark the end of this world. So let’s turn to the Scriptures and see what they reveal.

A New Temple

The Bible hints at a future temple, a place where God’s people will worship Him again.

Then there was given to me a measuring rod like a staff; and someone said, “Get up and measure the temple of God and the altar, and those who worship in it.”
– Revelation 11:1 NASB

This vision shows a time when God’s temple will be central again, a sign of His presence among His people.

The First Half of the Week: A Time of Peace

The final week may begin with a promise of peace, but it won’t last. A leader will make a covenant, perhaps offering hope to many.

Then he shall confirm a covenant with many for one week; but in the middle of the week he shall bring an end to sacrifice and offering. And on the wing of abominations shall be one who makes desolate, even until the consummation, which is determined, is poured out on the desolate.
– Daniel 9:27 NKJV
While they are saying, “Peace and safety!” then sudden destruction will come upon them like labor pains upon a pregnant woman, and they will not escape.
– 1 Thessalonians 5:3 NASB

The Middle of the Week: A Turning Point

In the middle of this week, the peace will break. Sacrifices will stop, and a time of trouble will begin.

And he will confirm a covenant with the many for one week, but in the middle of the week he will put a stop to sacrifice and grain offering; and on the wing of abominations will come the one who makes desolate, until a complete destruction, one that is decreed, gushes forth on the one who makes desolate.
– Daniel 9:27 NASB

The Second Half of the Week: A Time of Trial

The second half of the week will bring challenges, as the world turns against God’s people.

Leave out the courtyard which is outside the temple and do not measure it, because it has been given to the nations; and they will trample the holy city for forty-two months.
– Revelation 11:2 NASB

God will also send two witnesses to speak His truth during this time, calling people to turn to Him.

And I will grant authority to my two witnesses, and they will prophesy for 1,260 days (3½ years), clothed in sackcloth.
– Revelation 11:3 NASB

The two witnesses will face great opposition, but God will show His power through them.

When they have finished their testimony, the beast that comes up out of the abyss will make war with them, and overcome them and kill them. And their dead bodies will lie on the street of the great city which spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt, where also their Lord was crucified. Those from the peoples, tribes, languages, and nations will look at their dead bodies for three and a half days, and will not allow their dead bodies to be laid in a tomb. And those who live on the earth will rejoice over them and celebrate; and they will send gifts to one another, because these two prophets tormented those who live on the earth. And after the three and a half days, the breath of life from God came into them, and they stood on their feet; and great fear fell upon those who were watching them.
– Revelation 11:7-11 NASB

Even in dark times, God’s power will shine, showing that He is always in control.

The End of the Week: Jesus Returns

At the end of the week, Jesus will return in glory, bringing His kingdom forever.

The Lord’s Reign Begins

The Seventh Trumpet

Then the seventh angel sounded; and there were loud voices in heaven, saying, “The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord and of His Christ; and He will reign forever and ever.”
– Revelation 11:15 NASB
Behold, I am telling you a mystery; we will not all sleep, but we will all be changed, in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trumpet; for the trumpet will sound, and the dead will be raised imperishable, and we will be changed.
– 1 Corinthians 15:51-52 NASB
For the Lord Himself will descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel and with the trumpet of God, and the dead in Christ will rise first.
– 1 Thessalonians 4:16 NASB
And He will send forth His angels with a great trumpet blast, and they will gather together His elect from the four winds, from one end of the sky to the other.
– Matthew 24:31 NASB

When Jesus returns, He will gather His people and reign forever, fulfilling every promise.

Stay Watchful

God calls us to stay alert, ready for His return, because He loves us and wants us to be with Him.

– Luke 19:44 NASB

Israel didn’t see Jesus as the Messiah when He came, and they faced great loss. Let’s learn from this and keep our hearts open.

God Calls Us to Be Ready

Blessed are those slaves whom the master will find on the alert when he comes; truly I say to you, that he will prepare himself to serve, and have them recline at the table, and he will come up and serve them.
– Luke 12:37 NASB
Therefore be on the alert, for you do not know which day your Lord is coming.
– Matthew 24:42 NASB

Jesus asks us to stay awake and ready, trusting Him each day, because He will come back at an unexpected time.

The world may seem to offer peace, like recent efforts to bring nations together, but true peace comes only from Jesus. When He returns, everyone will see His glory.

– 1 Thessalonians 4:17-18 NASB

When Jesus returns, He will bring us to Himself, and we will be with Him forever. This hope keeps us strong, no matter what we face in the world.

A Pattern of Two’s: God’s Timing

God’s Word shows a pattern that points to His perfect timing, often in twos, reminding us to trust His plan.

Two Thousand Years Since Jesus’ Resurrection

It’s been about 2,000 years since Jesus rose from the dead, a sign that His return may be near.

But do not let this one fact escape your notice, beloved, that with the Lord one day is like a thousand years, and a thousand years like one day.
– 2 Peter 3:8 NASB
He will revive us after two days; He will raise us up on the third day, that we may live before Him.
– Hosea 6:2 NASB

These verses suggest that after a time like “two days” in God’s eyes, He will bring new life to His people.

Lazarus Raised After Two Days

Jesus waited two days before raising Lazarus, showing His power over death and His perfect timing.

So when He heard that he was sick, He then stayed two days longer in the place where He was. Then after this He said to the disciples, “Let’s go to Judea again.” The disciples said to Him, “Rabbi, the Jews were just now seeking to stone You, and yet You are going there again?” Jesus replied, “Are there not twelve hours in the day? If anyone walks during the day, he does not stumble, because he sees the light of this world. But if anyone walks during the night, he stumbles, because the light is not in him.” This He said, and after this He said to them, “Our friend Lazarus has fallen asleep; but I am going so that I may awaken him from sleep.”
– John 11:6-11 NASB

Jesus’ Resurrection on the Third Day

Jesus Himself rose on the third day, fulfilling God’s promise of victory over death.

Saying that the Son of Man must be handed over to sinful men, and be crucified, and on the third day rise from the dead.
– Luke 24:7 NASB

Returning After Two Days’ Wages

In a parable, Jesus spoke of a man who gave two days’ wages and promised to return.

On the next day he took out two denarii and gave them to the innkeeper and said, ‘Take care of him; and whatever more you spend, when I return, I will repay you.’
– Luke 10:35 NASB
